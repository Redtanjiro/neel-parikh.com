Case study — Brand & campaign

EMF ACE
Abu Dhabi.

An eleven-year-old wedding-industry brand's biggest event yet — teaser to gala, built inside a colour story and a logo lockup I didn't get to choose.

The constraint that shaped everything

Every post had to carry a colour story and two logos I didn't choose.

EMF Global runs on an eleven-year-old identity. The brief was specific about colour, and specific about where both marks — the EMF Global logo and the ACE Abu Dhabi lockup built for this edition — had to sit on every single asset, from a teaser nobody could yet identify as EMF to the gala night finale. There was no version of this project where I got to propose a new palette or redraw the mark.

That's a different job to Futee or CSEDS, where the system started from nothing. Here, the room to make calls was inside those fixed elements — what could carry zero branding and what couldn't, how a five-person panel card could hold a completely different density of information than a single-message announcement and still read as the same campaign.

The sequencing decision

Teaser, reveal, hype, credibility, event — the order was the design.

Nothing in this campaign is a one-off post. Every asset belongs to a phase, and the phase is what tells a stranger whether to keep scrolling or start paying attention.

What I killed

The desert nearly carried the whole campaign. It didn't.

An early direction leaned hard into desert imagery — dunes, camels, the open road. Most of it got killed in favour of the Arabian-night treatment that carries the rest of the campaign: deep purple, gold, a starlit sky over the Abu Dhabi skyline. Desert survived only where it started — the teaser phase, before there was a logo to carry.

The catch: this exact teaser went out for review with the wrong dates before it got caught — 20–21 July instead of the actual 26th–27th–28th. Same composition, same camel, same F1 car in the sand; the date block and the branding are the only things that changed.

The panel beat

One card holding five names instead of one message.

Most of the campaign is a single message per post — a date, a venue, a guest. "Global Design Fusion" is different: five real panelists, each with a title and a country, on one card, under the same two logos and the same colour system as everything else — carrying a completely different job.

What this taught me

Running it was the actual lesson.

Futee and CSEDS taught me to design a system from zero. EMF ACE taught me to run one that already existed — producing 1,000+ individual assets while coordinating production, product owners, and sponsors at the same time, not just a design file. That's a different skill to making one post look good, and it's the one this case study is actually about.
