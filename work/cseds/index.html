CSEDS

A digital home for 30 years of engineering

01 Overview

Thirty years of expertise, no digital front door.

CSEDS Australia is led by Ted Bennett — a builder for thirty years, now a chartered engineering consultant. The business pairs deep on-site experience with a curated portfolio of innovative construction products from leading manufacturers.

But all that credibility lived offline. The task: give CSEDS a website that felt as trustworthy and precise as their engineering — a place to explain the products, prove the expertise, and open a line to new clients.

The challenge

A specialist B2B business with a complex product range and decades of authority — but no website to carry it. It needed to communicate trust, technical credibility, and a clear path to a quote, without feeling like a generic construction template.

The solution

A clean, engineering-led site built in Framer: a confident hero, a structured product system, the founder's story front and centre, and clear calls to action — designed to convert visiting specifiers into conversations.

02 Approach

Designing for trust in a technical field.

Specifiers and builders don't buy from flashy sites — they buy from credibility. Every design decision leaned toward clarity and authority: room for the engineering story, a product system that's easy to scan, and a tone that reads like a trusted advisor, not a catalogue.

03 Visual System

Structural, precise, and grounded.

The palette borrows from the material world CSEDS works in — deep structural navy, steel blue-grey, and a construction orange that signals action. Confident display type carries the headlines; clean body type keeps the technical detail readable.

05 The Founder's Story

Credibility, made the centrepiece.

In a trust-driven field, the founder is the brand. The site puts Ted Bennett forward — thirty years as a builder, now a chartered engineer — with his own words carrying the philosophy:

"Good engineering isn't about the cheapest spec — it's about specifying the right product for the site, the load, and the next thirty years."
